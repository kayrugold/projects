1. Breaching the Integer Limit

Standard JavaScript integers break down after $2^{53}$. To hunt true prime patterns, we needed to go bigger.

Gnomonicus is my answer to the "Invisible Wall." It is a custom rendering engine built from scratch to handle BigInt arithmetic directly in the viewport. It treats numbers not as values, but as coordinate addresses in an infinite triangular lattice.

Engine Specs

  • Infinite Zoom: Pinch-to-zoom from 40x to 250x scale.
  • Volume Sums: Calculates the "weight" of integer history instantly.
  • Touch Physics: Smooth panning across infinite grid space.

2. Why It Matters

Most visualizers crash when you ask them to render the number $10^{50}$. Gnomonicus does not. By visualizing the "Tape ID" and "Phase ID" of these massive numbers, we can spot geometric anomalies that pure algebra hides.
